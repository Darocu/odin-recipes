Spam Musubi

Indulge in the irresistible flavors of Spam Musubi, a mouthwatering Hawaiian delight where savory slices of grilled Spam meet perfectly seasoned rice,

A simple recipe that combines the rich, salty goodness of Spam with a bed of seasoned rice, all tightly bound together by a strip of nori seaweed.

Spam is commonly pan-fried or grilled to achieve a nice crispy exterior.

The final result is both a savory and satisfying mouthwatering treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of Spam (usually 12 ounces)
  • 2 cups of sushi rice (short-grain rice)
  • 5 sheets of nori seaweed
  • Soy sauce
  • Furikake
  • Nori seaweed strips for wrapping
  • (Optional) Spam Musubi Mold or the empty Spam can with the bottom removed

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • Cook the rice according to the package instructions. Typically, you'll use 2 cups of water for every cup of rice. Allow the rice to cool to room temperature.
  2. Prepare the Rice:
    • Open the can of Spam and slice it into approximately 1/4-inch thick slices.
    • You can fry the Spam slices in a non-stick pan over medium heat until they're crispy on both sides. You can also grill them for extra flavor.
  3. Season the Rice:
    • While the rice is still warm, you can season it with a sprinkle of furikake, a Japanese rice seasoning, for added flavor. Mix it into the rice gently.
  4. Prepare the Nori Sheets:
    • Cut the nori sheets into strips about 1 inch wide. You'll use these to wrap the Spam Musubi.
  5. Assemble the Spam Musubi:
    • Place the Spam Musubi mold (or Spam can with the bottom removed) on a clean surface.
    • Lay a strip of plastic wrap or a piece of plastic food wrap inside the mold for easy removal.
    • Place a strip of nori seaweed on the plastic wrap inside the mold.
    • Add a layer of rice into the mold, pressing it down gently to create a solid base.
    • Lay a slice of cooked Spam on top of the rice.
    • Add another layer of rice on top of the Spam and press it down gently.
    • Fold the nori strip over the top of the rice and press it to seal the Musubi.
  6. Remove from Mold:
    • Carefully lift the Spam Musubi out of the mold using the plastic wrap.
  7. Serve and Enjoy:
    • You can serve Spam Musubi as is or wrap it in plastic wrap for a portable snack.
    • It's often enjoyed with a drizzle of soy sauce for extra flavor.
